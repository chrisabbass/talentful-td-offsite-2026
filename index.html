⚡ Activity

Beautiful Basics Portfolio Health Check

Step 1 · Pick One Client (1 min)

Choose an account with complexity, risk, ramping activity, reporting gaps, or room to tighten the rhythm.

Step 2 · Individual Reflection (5 min)

Assess against each BB category. What's fully in place? Where do we have the biggest opportunity to tighten? What gap creates the most risk? What do I tighten in the next 30 days?

Step 3 · Pair Share (10 min)

Which gap is most common? Which creates the most value when consistently done well? Where do you need better consistency from TLs? Where do you need to be more hands-on?

Step 4 · Group Discussion (10–15 min)

Share themes. Which BB has the biggest impact when done well? Which creates the most risk when missed? What should be non-negotiable?

✅ Output: Each TD leaves with one Beautiful Basics gap to close in the next 30 days.

Performance Management

TDs See It First and Act First

Performance management is how TDs identify risk early, diagnose root cause, and create clear action plans.

  • Performance starts with visibility — know where every consultant and account stands
  • Talentverse helps us diagnose what's driving the numbers
  • TDs move quickly when risk appears — clear plans, clear ownership, clear timelines
  • TDs coach, support, and hold the standard — accountability and support together
PerformanceClient SatEngagement
Principle 1

Performance Starts With Visibility

TDs should be able to answer these questions weekly.

Consultant Level

  • Who is on target? Who is behind?
  • Where is activity strong? Where is it dropping?
  • Who is converting well? Where is submission quality a concern?

Account Level

  • Is the client tracking to hiring goal?
  • Is the forecast reliable?
  • Where are process bottlenecks?

Principle 3

TDs Act Early and Create Clear Action Plans

Move quickly when risk appears. Clarity creates momentum.

Every action plan includes:

  • What changes — the specific adjustment or intervention
  • Who owns it — the TD, the TL, the consultant
  • What support is needed — from VP, from the client
  • When we review — the follow-up cadence
  • How we report it — the narrative for the client and leadership
  • When we escalate — the trigger for VP visibility
Principle 4

TDs Coach, Support, and Hold the Standard

Accountability and support go together.

  • Set the standard — make expectations clear for every consultant and TL
  • Review the data — use Talentverse weekly to spot trends early
  • Coach — give clear, practical support that helps team members improve
  • Be clear on what needs to change and what support is being provided
Clear expectations create engagement. When consultants know exactly what success looks like and how they're tracking, they are more likely to perform and stay engaged.

Weekly Rhythm

Talentverse Performance Review — Weekly Checklist

What every TD reviews each week.

  • Consultant data logged by EOD Friday
  • Performance vs. target reviewed for every consultant
  • Client progress vs. hiring target checked
  • Forecast and goal accuracy validated
  • Activity trends, pipeline, pass-through, time in stage reviewed
  • CSAT/NPS logged and reviewed
  • Below-target and low-data accounts flagged for action
Risk Indicators

⚠️ Consultant-Level Performance Signals

Patterns that tell TDs where to lean in.

Consistently below target
Low weekly activity
Missing or incomplete weekly inputs
Low submission volume
Weak conversion at key funnel stages
Candidates aging in pipeline
Stakeholder concerns flagged
Early signs of disengagement
Risk Indicators

⚠️ Account-Level Performance Signals

Portfolio-wide patterns that need TD action.

Client below 90% to hiring target
Forecast below target with no recovery plan
Goals inaccurate or outdated
Funnel too light to deliver
Drop-off at specific stages
Slow client feedback or process delays
Roles aging without movement
Client sentiment shifting or NPS/CSAT at risk
