Reporting

Before You Hit Send

Is the story clear?

  • Can the client quickly understand where we are against goal?
  • Does the report explain what changed since the last update?
  • Is the key message obvious without needing extra context?

Is there a point of view?

  • Are we interpreting the data, not just sharing it?
  • Are momentum, risks, blockers, or trends clearly called out?
  • Does the report explain what the data means for the client?

Is the action clear?

  • Are next steps specific?
  • Are owners and timelines included?
  • Are client asks clearly stated?

Does it build confidence?

  • Does the report feel proactive and thoughtful?
  • Does it show we know the account?
  • Would the client feel clear on what is happening and what comes next?

Performance Management

Performance Management Starts With Rhythm

Accountability is easier when the rhythm is clear. Before performance becomes an issue, TDs should have rituals in place that make expectations, progress, blockers, and next steps visible.

Strong rhythms might include:

Weekly Talentverse data review

Consultant self-review before 1:1s

TL performance check-ins

Client / account health reviews

Written feedback follow-ups

Clear weekly action commitments

When the rhythm is strong, you spend less time chasing and more time coaching, supporting, and leading.
Performance Management

TDs Own the Accountability,
Individuals Own the Action Plan

When performance is off track, TDs should be able to clearly answer:

What is happening? What does Talentverse show?
What needs to change? Is this activity, conversion, client process, goal accuracy, or consultant performance?
What has the individual committed to? What plan have they created? What will they do differently?
Where is it documented? What feedback, expectation, and follow-up date has been shared in writing?
What happens next? What support, escalation, or client action is needed?
TD ownership means setting the standard, creating clarity, and holding follow-through — not carrying the plan alone.
